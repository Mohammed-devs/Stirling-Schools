Admission Process Overview

Joining Stirling Schools is a straightforward process designed to ensure the right fit for each student.

1

Submit Application

Complete and submit the application form with required documents to our Admissions Office. Applications are accepted from April until the beginning of June.

2

Assessment & Interview

All students must undergo a level test and an individual interview as part of the admission process to assess academic ability, English language proficiency, and fit.

3

Admission Decision

After reviewing assessment results and previous school records, we will notify families of our admission decision as soon as possible.

4

Enrollment & Payment

Once accepted, families must pay the enrollment fee within one week and complete remaining payments by the specified dates to secure their child's place.

Admissions Criteria

Our admissions committee reviews applications based on several key criteria to ensure students will thrive in our educational environment.

Age

Children reach compulsory school age at the beginning of the term following their 6th birthday. Any child whose birthday falls between 1st December and 31st January is eligible to apply in the previous scholastic year.

Availability of Places

We encourage parents to inform the school at an early stage of their wish to seek admission as there is a high demand for places that swiftly become unavailable.

English Language Proficiency

Students' English language skills are assessed as part of the admission process. Those without adequate English may be offered a preparatory year.

Previous School Record

We review academic records from previous schools to assess a student's academic history and readiness for our program.

Enrollment Age Guide

Enrollment Interview and Assessment

All students must undergo a level test and an individual interview to be admitted. As a part of the application procedure, parents are informed about the school's education system.

Application Review

After receiving the completed application form, our admissions committee will review the application according to our admissions criteria, including availability of places, age, academic ability, English language proficiency, previous school record, behavior, and maturity of the candidate.

Assessment Process

The level test assesses the student's academic abilities and knowledge, with a focus on core subjects. The individual interview helps us understand the student's personality, interests, and goals, while also allowing us to address any questions or concerns.

Notification and Enrollment

After reviewing the assessment results and previous school records, the school will announce its final decision regarding admission. Families will be notified as soon as possible of the outcome, and if a place is offered, they must pay the enrollment fee within one week to secure it.

Admission Checklist

This checklist will assist you in completing the application process in a timely and efficient way.

  • Official copies of academic records from the previous school
  • Duly completed application form
  • A photocopy of the student's passport (if available)
  • A photocopy of the student's birth certificate to confirm date of birth
  • Four identical passport-sized head-and-shoulders photographs in color
  • A health report
  • A guarantee letter from your company if they will be covering the cost of school enrollment fees and tuition fees

Note: The enrollment fee and other fees will need to be paid by the times specified in the application procedure. If you are making an application during term-time, all fees will need to be paid before the student can join the school.

SSAT Admission Test

As Stirling Schools, we accept students for intake at certain levels. For grades 6 and 9, we have a standard school admission test, usually administered at the beginning of May.

Test Format

This test features Mathematics, Logic, Science, and English multiple-choice questions designed to assess a student's academic readiness for our program.

Scholarship Opportunities

Depending on the result of the test, students may be offered scholarships to help cover the cost of their studies at Stirling Schools. High-performing students can receive significant financial assistance.

Preparatory Year

Students who do not have an adequate level of English will be offered a preparatory year to develop their language skills before joining the regular program.
