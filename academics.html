Our Education Model

Stirling Schools Education Model (SEM 速)

Stirling Schools has designed and implements a unique education model that combines rich components, skills, and standards to raise engaged, reliable, and confident citizens of the global community. We deliver quality education to help students develop into critical thinkers who are motivated to be independent, creative and entrepreneurial in their contributions to society.

We support our students' academic potential while simultaneously instilling authenticity, self-esteem and strong ethical values within every individual.

Competencies

We focus on developing essential competencies in our students, including Communication, Collaboration, Creativity, Critical Thinking, Citizenship, Character, and more. These are the skills that are essential for students to succeed in school, higher education, careers, and life.

Coherent Education Framework

We implement a coherent education model with a digital curriculum framework that is coherently sequenced, deliberately designed to facilitate learning, free of academic inconsistencies, and aligned across lessons, courses, subject areas, and grade levels.

International Standards

Our educational standards meet international criteria including recognized student certificates, a multinational and multilingual student body, an accredited education program, international accreditation, multinational teacher population, English/bilingual instruction, and strong emphasis on extracurricular activities.

Culturally Responsive Curriculum

Stirling Schools educational standards meet the requirements of local contexts, culture(s), and national assessments. We believe in mother tongue education as a fundamental element of the foundation of learning, while promoting civic competence and traditional values.

Digitized Education

Our education is supported with quality and rich resources including international books, digital and online resources, classroom technology such as smart boards, and various supportive resources to enhance the learning experience.

Character Education

Stirling Schools believes in the importance of focusing on universal values which are essential to growing academically successful individuals with good character. Our motto is 'World Peace through Personal Peace', and we implement a comprehensive values curriculum.

Enriched Education Model (EEM 速)

As its motto, "Reimagining Education" suggests, Stirling Schools believes that education needs to be enriched in order to keep up with the ever-changing world. We integrate the Enriched Education Model into our dynamic educational services.

What is EEM 速?

Enriched Education Model is a general name for an educational program that:

  • Aims to help students explore their potential
  • Teaches how to learn, and promotes learner autonomy
  • Teaches while entertaining, and entertains while teaching
  • Enriches uniform education

EEM 速 Applies to 4 Fields

Enrichment in PE, Art, Music, and Computer Classes

Enhancing physical, artistic, musical, and technological education with creative approaches and activities.

Enrichment with Extracurricular Courses

Providing additional learning opportunities beyond the standard curriculum to develop varied interests and skills.

Enrichment through Values Education

Implementing a comprehensive values curriculum to develop character and ethical foundations.

Enrichment in Core Subjects

Enhancing the teaching of main academic subjects with innovative methods and resources.

Curriculum Overview

The Stirling Schools curriculum provides positive support for students to achieve their goals by teaching them to take responsibility for their own learning.

Diversity

We have designed our curriculum to fit a diverse learning environment in which students can discover their own individual capacities and interests.

Challenging

Our curriculum offers more than just the teaching of core subjects. It includes the teaching of a foreign language, activities and projects, national and international assessment possibilities, technology, robotics, brain games, and values teaching.

Our curriculum is a dynamic program which is reviewed and updated annually for each section on our Curriculum Development Day, an event which is organized with the participation of our Heads of Department and educational experts, and where feedback from students' surveys is also included.

Stirling Schools offer their students international educational traditions, as well as the benefits of access to the rich culture and heritage of Iraq and Kurdistan. We collaboratively develop and continue to implement rich curricula based on clear measurable learning goals.

Primary School Curriculum

Primary education at Stirling Schools is divided into Key Stage 1 (Grades 1-3) and Key Stage 2 (Grades 4-6), with a focus on developing foundational skills, independence, and a love for learning.

Key Stage 1 (Grades 1-3)

In Grades 1, 2 and 3, pupils have their classroom and class teacher for the majority of their lessons. The class teacher develops a strong relationship with pupils and their families and acts as the main point of contact for home-school communications.

We offer a rigorous educational program that promotes well-rounded development: learning with head, heart, and hands. Grade level teachers continue with a class from one year to the next, ideally from Grade 1 to Grade 3, forming a community that works through challenges and celebrations together.

Literacy starts our day with a 15-minute reading session every morning. Our Numeracy lessons are designed with fun in mind, as we impart new concepts and show how these relate to the real world through game-based learning and technology.

Key Stage 2 (Grades 4-6)

Pupils in grades 4, 5 and 6 are taught by specialist subject teachers, recognizing their growing independence and the higher challenge of learning. However, as primary age children who still need care and support, they also benefit from having a homeroom teacher responsible for their welfare.

Our students are vibrant, enthusiastic individuals who are regarded as role models. We have established a positive community of learners accustomed to working collaboratively. Pupils are encouraged to demonstrate independence and are given a voice and choice in their learning.

We organize many enjoyable activities including Spelling Bees, Handwriting competitions, Counting contests, Spring Festivals, Science festivals, Cultural festivals, and Educational field trips.

English Language Teaching

Our comprehensive English language program is aligned with international standards and supported by internationally recognized examinations.

CEFR Alignment

We base the English language teaching that we deliver on the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR). We have determined the level of each grade based on the CEFR and set exit band targets for our students accordingly.

We aim to make sure that every student reaches the appropriate exit band level by the time they complete each grade. In addition to our internal assessment, we use Cambridge and TOEFL international examinations to assess students' English language proficiency.

Cambridge Exams

Cambridge YLE

Cambridge English: Young Learners, also known as Young Learners English Tests, is a suite of English language examinations specially designed for children in primary schools. In Stirling Schools, students take these exams at the primary level.

Cambridge Key

Key for Schools (formerly KET for Schools), is an exam for school-age learners which helps prepare them for higher-level English language qualifications. At Stirling Schools, students take this exam at the end of the preparatory year program or in grade 7.

Cambridge Preliminary

Preliminary (formerly PET), is the English language exam that shows students have mastered the basics. At Stirling Schools, students take this exam at the end of grade 7 or in grade 10, as appropriate to their needs.

TOEFL Exams

TOEFL Primary

The TOEFL Primary program offers tests for students ages 8+ to measure a range of skills. Each test offers reliable administration and scoring, with meaningful feedback to help young learners progress. The tests are offered at two levels of proficiency, Step 1 and Step 2.

TOEFL Junior

The TOEFL Junior速 tests, intended for students ages 11+ in middle school and lower levels of high school, accurately measure students' communicative skills in English, helping to pinpoint strengths and challenges.

TOEFL ITP

The TOEFL ITP program provides a convenient, affordable and reliable method to evaluate students and curricula of English language programs. It is administered locally by institutions for defined purposes, such as providing internationally benchmarked achievement standards.

Special Programs

We offer specialized programs to support our students in achieving their educational goals and preparing for their futures.

Wezari Exam Preparation

Success in the Wezari exam is the outcome of our students' 12-year educational journey. Our most experienced teachers take Wezari Exam classes, and the guidance department supports students with advice on stress management and extra social activities to help them cope with this critical year.

Our preparation programs include:

  • Zero Mistake, B.A.L, and Doping programs
  • Regular mock exams to evaluate progress
  • Question Banks prepared by experienced teachers
  • Extra study sessions during holidays

As a result of this detailed preparation, Stirling School students outperform others and are admitted to prestigious faculties including Medicine, Dentistry, Pharmacy, Engineering, and Education at national and international universities.

International University Preparation

SAT Preparation

The College Board Scholastic Aptitude Test (SAT) and SAT Subject Tests are officially recognized by Kurdistan and Iraqi Educational Authorities for International Schools. International school students can be admitted to local and international universities with their SAT results.

Our international schools provide a well-balanced curriculum that prepares students for these tests, with SAT Support programs delivered by experienced teachers. Students' progress is monitored carefully, and they regularly sit SAT and SAT Subject Mock Tests.

AP Preparation

Advanced Placement examinations (AP exams) are standardized exams designed to measure how well students have mastered the content and skills of a specific AP course. Some colleges and universities grant credits or advanced placement based on AP test scores.

Stirling international schools provide elective AP courses at grade 12. Students benefit from support programs delivered by experienced teachers, and their progress is monitored carefully with regular AP Mock Tests.

College Admission Counselling

Our College Admission Counsellor Service assists high school students, especially 12th-grade students, with academic planning and university admissions. Services include guidance on university preparation, information on admissions tests and scholarships, application support, and career planning.
